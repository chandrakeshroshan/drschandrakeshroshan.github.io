About Me

I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Physics at IIT Madras, working under the supervision of Dr. Pankaj Jaiswal. My research focuses on studying ferroelectric phase transitions using molecular dynamics simulations, with a particular emphasis on understanding the thermodynamic properties and transition mechanisms between ferroelectric and paraelectric phases.

With a strong background in computational physics and theoretical condensed matter physics, I employ advanced simulation techniques to analyze temperature-driven structural transformations in ferroelectric materials. My work combines rigorous theoretical frameworks with high-performance computing to advance our understanding of these complex materials systems.

Current Focus

My current research involves developing computational methods for phase transition analysis and investigating the microscopic mechanisms that drive ferroelectric-paraelectric transitions. I use LAMMPS for molecular dynamics simulations and employ various visualization tools to understand the complex behavior of these materials at the atomic scale.
