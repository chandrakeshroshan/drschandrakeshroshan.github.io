About Me

Assistant Professor in Physics

Rajiv Gandhi University of Knowledge Technologies (RGUKT), Basar

Dr. S Chand Rakesh Roshan is an Assistant Professor in Physics at Rajiv Gandhi University of Knowledge Technologies (RGUKT), Basar, where he has been serving since October 2010. He earned his Ph.D. from the National Institute of Technology Warangal in 2024, with research focused on computational physics and materials science.

Dr. Roshan's research expertise lies in lattice thermal conductivity, DFT calculations, and materials modeling. He has published over 10 research papers in prestigious journals including ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces, Inorganic Chemistry, and ACS Applied Energy Materials. His work on highly anisotropic materials and ultralow thermal conductivity has gained significant recognition in the materials science community.

Beyond research, Dr. Roshan is passionate about educational technology and has served as the SWAYAM-NPTEL Coordinator and HEAD of University Training and Placements. He is a certified Microsoft Innovative Educator Trainer, Adobe Creative Educator, and Prezi Certified Educator Trainer. He has authored four books on physics and computational simulations and is a prolific speaker at national and international conferences.

About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics at Rajiv Gandhi University of Knowledge Technologies (RGUKT), Basar, where I have been contributing to research and education since October 2010.

My research focuses on computational materials science, particularly investigating thermal transport properties using first-principles calculations. I specialize in studying lattice thermal conductivity, phonon dynamics, and structural phase transitions in various material systems including layered materials, rocksalt compounds, and alkaline-earth chalcogenides.

Research Interests

  • Computational Materials Science using Density Functional Theory (DFT)
  • Lattice Thermal Conductivity and Phonon Transport
  • High-Pressure Physics and Structural Phase Transitions
  • Thermoelectric Materials and Energy Materials
  • Layered Materials (PbClF-type, Bi2O2S) and 2D Materials
  • Alkaline-Earth Chalcogenides and Halides

Research

Computational Materials Science

First-principles calculations using VASP and Quantum ESPRESSO for investigating electronic structure and properties of materials.

Thermal Transport

Lattice thermal conductivity in layered materials and compounds, focusing on highly anisotropic materials and ultralow thermal conductivity for thermoelectric applications.

High-Pressure Physics

Structural phase transitions under extreme conditions, investigating anharmonicity and Raman spectra under high pressure.

Educational Technology

Integration of ICT tools in physics education, development of digital learning resources, and training educators on innovative teaching methodologies.

Key Research Contributions

  • Lattice thermal conductivity in layered PbClF-type materials
  • Effect of atomic mass contrast on thermal transport in materials
  • Pressure-induced phase transitions in various compounds
  • Materials design for low thermal conductivity applications
  • Development of computational physics laboratory and resources
